## Sharing and Evaluation

> These are my personal experiences and feedback about the **AWS Workforce Bootcamp - First Cloud AI Journey** program, aiming to help improve the program quality for future interns.

---

### Detailed Evaluation

#### 1. Work Environment

The work environment at AWS is very professional and dynamic. Everyone is enthusiastic, ready to share knowledge and experience. What impressed me most is the "continuous learning" culture - no one is afraid to ask questions or admit when they don't know something.

The offline meetups and hands-on workshops were very beneficial, giving interns the opportunity to meet, exchange ideas directly, and build better relationships with each other and with mentors.

#### 2. Mentor and Team Support

My mentor was always patient in answering questions, from basic issues to complex cases. The strength is that the mentor doesn't just "give fish" but "teaches how to fish" - always guiding me to research, debug, and find solutions before suggesting answers.

The admin team was also very supportive in providing documents, setting up lab environments, and resolving technical issues. Response time was quite fast, usually within a few hours.

#### 3. Relevance to Major

The bootcamp content aligns well with what I learned at school about Cloud Computing, Networking, and DevOps. Particularly, workshops on Infrastructure as Code (CDK, CloudFormation) helped me apply theory to practice.

The AI/ML part was quite new to me, but it's a good opportunity to expand my skillset into a trending field. I think there should be more foundational ML basics before diving into AWS AI services.

#### 4. Learning and Development Opportunities

This program helped me develop many skills:
- **Technical skills**: Mastered 20+ AWS services, understood cloud architecture and best practices
- **Soft skills**: Improved presentation skills (workshop demos), documentation writing, and time management
- **Problem-solving**: Learned to troubleshoot errors, read logs, and optimize cloud costs

Especially, the final project helped me consolidate knowledge and create a real product to add to my portfolio.

#### 5. Culture and Team Spirit

Fellow bootcamp participants supported each other, shared materials, and answered questions on the Slack channel. There was no negative competition but rather a spirit of learning from each other.

Mentors and the AWS team were also very approachable, creating no distance despite their greater experience. This helped me feel more confident when asking questions or discussing technical issues.

#### 6. Policies and Benefits

- **AWS Credit**: $200 credit for hands-on practice allowed me to experiment freely without worrying about costs
- **Certification voucher**: Support for AWS certification exam voucher was a great motivation
- **Learning resources**: Access to many courses on AWS Skill Builder and internal materials
- **Flexible time**: Flexible learning schedule suitable for students

However, additional allowance or transportation support for offline sessions would be better.

---

### Additional Questions

**What satisfied you most during the internship?**

I was most satisfied with the quality of training content and the opportunity to work on real projects. Getting hands-on with AWS services and building a complete solution from start to finish greatly boosted my confidence in working with cloud.

**What do you think the company needs to improve for future interns?**

- **Vietnamese documentation**: Some materials should have Vietnamese versions so those with weaker English can keep up
- **Prerequisite knowledge**: Should have a checklist of required knowledge before joining so participants can prepare better
- **Real-world projects**: If possible, let interns participate in actual company projects (even small tasks) to experience real work processes
- **Mentoring time**: Increase one-on-one time with mentors to discuss career paths more deeply

**Would you recommend your friends to intern here? Why?**

Yes, I would! Because:
- High-quality program, well-structured with updated technology
- Enthusiastic mentors, positive learning environment
- Certificate and credits for practice
- AWS brand on CV will be an advantage when applying for jobs

However, I would also remind friends that they need a proactive learning mindset and not be afraid to research/self-study, as the bootcamp pace is quite fast.

---

### Proposals and Expectations

**Proposals to improve the internship experience:**

1. **Better mentorship pairing**: Match interns with mentors based on interests and career goals
2. **Weekly Q&A sessions**: Have office hours each week for interns to ask questions together
3. **Peer review**: Encourage interns to review each other's code/architecture to learn
4. **Guest speaker series**: Invite AWS Solution Architects or Cloud Engineers to share about career paths
5. **Post-bootcamp support**: Create an alumni group for networking and knowledge updates after completion

**Do you want to continue working with AWS in the future?**

Yes! I would love to have the opportunity to work full-time at AWS or companies with similar culture. Through the 12-week bootcamp, I found that I fit well with Cloud and AI/ML fields, and AWS is an ideal place to develop my career in this direction.

If there's an intern-to-fulltime program or graduate program, I would be very interested in participating.

**Other feedback:**

- Should have a clearer roadmap about career paths in cloud (Cloud Engineer, Solutions Architect, DevOps, etc.) so interns can orient themselves
- Create an internal platform/blog for interns to share lessons learned and troubleshooting tips
- Organize a demo day at the end for interns to present their projects, possibly inviting other teams in the company to attend

---

**Summary:** This was a very valuable internship experience. I learned a lot of technical knowledge, soft skills, and had the opportunity to work in a professional environment. Thank you AWS team and FCAJ for creating this opportunity! 🙏
