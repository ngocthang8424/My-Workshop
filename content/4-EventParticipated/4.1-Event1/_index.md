# Summary Report: Student Meetup – AWS Services & Hackathon Program

### Event Objectives

- Create a peer-learning space where students share practical AWS knowledge and program experience.
- Introduce core AWS services and how they support real projects and learning paths.
- Present the Hackathon program: goals, timeline, team formation, and expectations for participants.
- Strengthen presentation skills and technical communication in English among student speakers.

### Speakers

- **Huynh Thai Linh** – HUTECH; opening speaker on AWS services and cloud learning orientation.
- **Nguyen Tran Minh Quan** – FPT University; student speaker (English presentation) on the Hackathon program.
- **Huynh An Khuong** – FPT University; student speaker (English presentation) on the Hackathon program.
- **Mai Quoc Anh** – FPT University; student speaker (English presentation) on the Hackathon program.

### Key Highlights

#### Opening session – AWS services (Huynh Thai Linh, HUTECH)

- Overview of commonly used AWS services in learning and project work (compute, storage, networking, security).
- How AWS services connect to hands-on labs, workshops, and capstone-style projects in the bootcamp.
- Tips for choosing services by use case and avoiding common beginner mistakes (cost, permissions, region).

#### Student speaker segment – Hackathon program (English)

- **Nguyen Tran Minh Quan**, **Huynh An Khuong**, and **Mai Quoc Anh** (FPT University, same team) presented in English on the Hackathon program structure and participation flow.
- Topics typically covered: team roles, problem statement, submission criteria, and collaboration expectations.
- Emphasis on applying cloud skills from the program to a time-boxed, competitive project environment.

#### Meetup format – students as speakers

- Unlike a traditional vendor-led session, this meetup put **students on stage** as speakers, encouraging confidence and knowledge sharing across cohorts.
- Q&A and discussion between speakers and attendees helped clarify AWS learning paths and Hackathon preparation.

### Key Takeaways

#### AWS learning

- Clearer map of which AWS services to prioritize at each stage of the bootcamp.
- Better understanding of how workshop content (VPC, IAM, serverless, IaC, etc.) fits into real project design.

#### Hackathon readiness

- Understood Hackathon objectives, expected deliverables, and how teams usually organize work.
- Noted the importance of English communication when presenting ideas and demoing solutions.

#### Soft skills

- Observed effective student-led presentation styles: structure, pacing, and audience engagement.
- Gained motivation to participate actively in community events and peer sharing sessions.

### Applying to Work

- Apply AWS service selection principles when designing the capstone architecture.
- Plan Hackathon participation with defined roles, milestones, and service boundaries aligned with bootcamp skills.
- Practice short technical presentations in English for demos and progress reviews.

### Event Experience

Attending the **Student Meetup** was a useful complement to weekly workshops. Highlights included:

#### Learning from peer speakers
- **Huynh Thai Linh** (HUTECH) provided a practical lens on AWS services beyond slide-level definitions.
- The three English sessions from **Nguyen Tran Minh Quan**, **Huynh An Khuong**, and **Mai Quoc Anh** made the Hackathon program concrete and actionable.

#### Community and networking
- Exchanged ideas with classmates about project ideas, service choices, and team formation for Hackathon.
- Saw how student-led events can reinforce bootcamp learning without waiting only for formal lectures.

#### Lessons learned
- Peer teaching reinforces my own understanding when preparing questions and follow-up notes.
- Hackathon success depends on clear scope, teamwork, and aligning architecture with available AWS skills and credits.
